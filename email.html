Email Spoofing - How to identify email
spoofing
  • Author Kadoski | 21st April, 2023.

It can be difficult to identify email spoofing, as the messages are often designed to look like they are legitimate. However, there are some common signs that may indicate that an email is spoofed:
1. The sender's email address does not match the name or domain of the sender. For example, an email claiming to be from a bank might have a sender's address that does not match the bank's domain name.
2. The message contains suspicious or unexpected content, such as requests for personal information or urgent requests to take action.
3. The message contains spelling or grammar errors that suggest it was not written by a professional.
4. The message contains links or attachments that appear suspicious or unexpected. These may lead to phishing websites or malware.
The message header information contains inconsistencies, such as different time zones or IP addresses that do not match the sender's location.
If you suspect that an email is spoofed, it is best to err on the side of caution and not click on any links or provide any personal information. You can also contact the purported sender of the email to verify its authenticity. Additionally, organizations can use email security tools such as SPF, DKIM, and DMARC to help detect and prevent email spoofing.
